To effectively provide products and services that meet your stakeholders’ and users’ real needs it is necessary to identify and involve all of the stakeholders. You must also identify the users of the system and ensure that the stakeholder community adequately represents them. 

This section provides a profile of the stakeholders and users involved in the project, and the key problems that they perceive to be addressed by the proposed solution. It does not describe their specific requests or requirements as these are captured in a separate stakeholder requests artifact. Instead, it provides the background and justification for why the requirements are needed.

### Example <hr>

### Primary Stakeholders

***Students and Young Adults (End Users)***
- **Description**: Individuals aged 15–25 who require support managing income, expenses, and savings.
- **Needs**: Simple budgeting tools, clear visualisations, secure login, and goal‑tracking features.
- **Success Criteria**: Ability to easily record transactions, understand spending behaviour, and track progress.

***Parents and Guardians***
- **Description**: Adults supporting young people in developing financial literacy.
- **Needs**: Confidence that the app is safe, secure, and educational.
- **Success Criteria**: Young person demonstrates improved budgeting habits.

## Secondary Stakeholders

***Educators***
- **Description**: Teachers or mentors integrating financial literacy into learning programs.
- **Needs**: Reliable, youth‑friendly tools that reinforce budgeting concepts.
- **Success Criteria**: Students engage with the app and demonstrate understanding of financial principles.

***Developers and Project Team***
- **Description**: Individuals responsible for designing, implementing, and maintaining the system.
- **Needs**: Clear requirements, modular architecture, and maintainable codebase.
- **Success Criteria**: System meets functional requirements, is secure, and can scale.
